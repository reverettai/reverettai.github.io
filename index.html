Research

I am broadly interested in (deep) multi-agent (reinforcement) learning. In recent years, I have become especially excited about human-AI interaction. For example, how can we efficiently train agents to interact well with humans? How can we ensure this interaction is both meaningful and cooperative? How can this be used to benefit society? Prior to this, my PhD thesis investigated the automation and influence of environment design on learned agent behaviours.

We introduce Fictitious Co-Play (FCP), a simple and intuitive training method for producing agents capable of zero-shot coordination with humans in Overcooked. FCP works by training an agent as the best response to a frozen pool of self-play agents and their past checkpoints. Notably, FCP exhibits robust generalization to humans, despite not using any human data during training.
